## The Basics: How Quantum Computers Work

Unlike classical computers, which use bits as the smallest unit of data, quantum computers use qubits. A qubit can exist in a state of 0, 1, or both simultaneously (thanks to superposition), and can be entangled with other qubits, allowing for complex computations to be performed more efficiently than with classical systems.
